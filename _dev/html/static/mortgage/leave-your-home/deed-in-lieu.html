If you're facing foreclosure and you're open to the idea of selling your home, a short sale may be the best option for you.

How a short sale works

A short sale is when you sell your house for less than what you owe on your mortgage. Your mortgage company agrees to accept the money you sell your house for—even though this amount is less than what you owe on your loan—and still consider your mortgage (or a portion of it) paid off.

Some benefits of a short sale include:

  • Paying off your mortgage for less than what you currently owe.
  • Listing your home at a competitive price so it can sell more quickly.
  • Avoiding foreclosure.

Are you eligible for a short sale?

A short sale may be a good option for you if:

  • You're willing to sell your home so you can move on and make a fresh start.
  • Your mortgage is "under water" or "upside down," meaning you owe more than your home is worth.
  • You're experiencing a long-term hardship.
  • You're behind on your mortgage payments.
  • You haven't qualified for a mortgage modification.

Next steps

Call your mortgage company to see if a short sale can work given your situation. To save time, gather this information before you call:

  • Your mortgage statement and other loan information such as payments and balances of second mortgages, automobiles or student loans.
  • Copies of your two most recent pay stubs showing your year-to-date earnings.
  • If you're self-employed or an independent contractor, your most recent Quarterly or Year-to-Date Profit/Loss statement.
  • Your most recent personal checking, savings, money market, mutual funds, stock and bond statements.
  • Recent tax returns.

Be ready to explain your financial circumstances when you call. Your mortgage representative is there to help you achieve the best possible outcome for your situation.
