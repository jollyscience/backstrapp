If you're behind on one or two mortgage payments because you were going through a short-term financial hardship that is now over, an extension may bring your account up to date.

How an extension works

An extension is designed for homeowners who experienced a temporary hardship that is now resolved. Here's how it works:

  • Your lender agrees to move the payments you missed to the end of your mortgage term, usually a maximum of two months per extension.
  • Your account will be considered up to date.
  • You mortgage term will be extended to include the payments you missed.

Are you eligible for an extension?

Here are some general eligibility criteria for an extension:

  • You experienced a temporary financial hardship that has been resolved.
  • You've made one full mortgage payment within 30 days of requesting the extension.
  • You're no more than two payments behind on your mortgage.
  • You'll be able to make your regularly scheduled mortgage payments for the extended term of your mortgage.

Next steps

If you're behind on mortgage payments, contact your mortgage company as soon as possible. If you don't qualify for an extension, there may be another solution available.

Before you call, save time by gathering this information:

  • Your mortgage statement and other loan information such as payments and balances of second mortgages, automobiles or student loans.
  • Copies of your two most recent pay stubs showing your year-to-date earnings.
  • If you're self-employed or an independent contractor, your most recent Quarterly or Year-to-Date Profit/Loss statement.
  • Your most recent personal checking, savings, money market, mutual funds, stock and bond statements.
  • Recent tax returns.

You'll also need to explain the hardship that kept you from making your mortgage payments. The more information you can provide, the better.
