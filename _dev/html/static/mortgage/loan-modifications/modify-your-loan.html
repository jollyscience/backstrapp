Maybe you need help because your financial circumstances have permanently changed and you can't afford your mortgage payments anymore. If your mortgage company can't refinance your mortgage, they may be able to make your payments more affordable with a mortgage modification.

How a mortgage modification works

If you qualify for a mortgage modification, the terms of your mortgage will permanently change to make it more manageable for your financial situation.

  • This could involve changing the length of your mortgage term, the type of interest rate you have or the interest rate itself.
  • You may wind up with a lower monthly mortgage payment.
  • Your account will be brought up to date so you can have a fresh start on payments.
  • Any overdue amount and escrow is added onto the new unpaid principal balance and distributed over the life of the loan.

Are you eligible for a loan modification?

The requirements for getting a loan modification vary depending on the exact type of modification. Ask your mortgage company about a mortgage modification if the following apply to you:

  • You're experiencing a long-term financial hardship due to circumstances such as divorce, reduced income, disability or serious illness.
  • You're behind on your mortgage payments or you're likely to fall behind soon.
  • You don't qualify for refinancing.

Next steps

If you feel like you can't afford your mortgage, it's definitely time to contact your mortgage company. Waiting too long can reduce your chances of finding a solution for your situation.

Gather this information before you call to talk about a mortgage modification:

  • Your mortgage statement and other loan information such as payments and balances of second mortgages, automobiles or student loans.
  • Copies of your two most recent pay stubs showing your year-to-date earnings.
  • If you're self-employed or an independent contractor, your most recent Quarterly or Year-to-Date Profit/Loss statement.
  • Your most recent personal checking, savings, money market, mutual funds, stock and bond statements.
  • Recent tax returns.

When you talk to your representative, explain your financial situation and why you can't afford your mortgage. This will help identify options that may be available to you.
