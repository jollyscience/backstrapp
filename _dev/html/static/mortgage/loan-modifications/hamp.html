Many homeowners simply can't afford their mortgages anymore because they're going through a long-term financial hardship. The U.S. government created HAMP to help these homeowners get into more affordable mortgages so they can stay in their homes.

How HAMP works

HAMP lowers your monthly mortgage payment to 31% of your monthly gross (pre-tax) income. Through HAMP, the terms of your existing mortgage will change and your account will be brought up to date as soon as the modification is finalized. Here's how the HAMP process works:

  • If your mortgage company says you qualify for HAMP, they'll send you a packet with some forms you'll need to fill out, sign and submit.
  • After you've been approved, you'll start a three-month HAMP trial period. During this period, you'll make your new, lower mortgage payments.
  • Upon successfully completing the trial period, you'll receive a final Modification Agreement to sign and your mortgage will be permanently modified.

Are you eligible for HAMP?

You may be eligible for HAMP if all of the following apply to you:

  • You're facing a long-term financial hardship due to circumstances such as divorce, reduced income, disability or serious illness.
  • You're behind on your mortgage payments or will be soon.
  • Your home is your primary residence.
  • Your existing mortgage was originated on or before January 1, 2009.
  • Your mortgage payment is more than 31% of your monthly income before taxes and other deductions.
  • You don't owe more than $729,750 on your home.
  • You'll be able to afford the modified payment every month.
  • Your mortgage company participates in the Home Affordable Modification Program. Find out here.

Next steps

First, contact your mortgage company and ask about HAMP. To save time, have the following documents handy before you call:

  • Your mortgage statement and other loan information such as payments and balances of second mortgages, automobiles or student loans.
  • Copies of your two most recent pay stubs showing your year-to-date earnings.
  • If you're self-employed or an independent contractor, your most recent Quarterly or Year-to-Date Profit/Loss statement.
  • Your most recent personal checking, savings, money market, mutual funds, stock and bond statements.
  • Recent tax returns.

Be ready to discuss your unique financial situation and why you can't afford your mortgage. This will help your representative find the best possible solution for your situation.
